Authors

Jeannette Walls

Jeannette Walls wrote The Glass Castle in 2005. She graduated from Barnard College and was a journalist in New York. She is the author of The Silver Star and Half Broke Horses, names one of the ten best books of 2009 by the editors of the New York Times Book Review. Walls lives in rural Virginia with her husband, the writer John Taylor. She is currently 64 years old.

Mary Karr

Mary Karr wrote The Liars' Club in 1995. Her two volumes of poetry are The Devil's Tour and Abacus. She has won two Pushcart Prizes for both poetry and essays, and her work appears in such magazines as The New Yorker, Esquire, The New York Times Magazine, Granta, Parnassus, Vogue and America Poetry Review. The Liars' Club won the PEN/Martha Albrand award for best first non-fiction, was a finalist for the National Book Critics Circle Award, and was fiven the Texas Institute of Letters Prize for best nonfiction. Her grants include the prestigious Whiting Writer's Award and the Bunting Fellowship from Radcliffe College. Mary Karr is the Peck Professor of Literature at Syracuse University and she lives in New York City. She is currently 69 years old.
