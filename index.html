My Fascination with Memoirs

I have always been a huge reader, starting as far back as I can remember. Prior to last semester, I would say that my favorite genre was historical fiction. However, in Spring 2024, I took a class called Writing Nonfiction for the Public (ENGH 486) at George Mason University. In that class, I learned that the term "nonfiction" is much more flexible and complex than at first blush. A person can be as committed as they want to be regarding their memory of events, but long term memory becomes less and less reliable over time. Therefore, necessarily, there is a degree of distortion of these facts. But does this make a story fictional, or any less nonfictional? In ENGH 486, I learned that there are different defintions of what truth is. Something that is true to me, like a moral or belief, may not be true to you, or anyone else.
